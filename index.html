The Non-Technical Manager's Guide to Engineering Teams

Sound familiar?

You inherited the team

Your technical lead left. Now the decisions nobody else understands land on your desk — in a language you can't fully read.

You built something that took off

You studied business, or operations — not engineering. Suddenly you're managing the people who write the code your company runs on.

You were brought in to fix it

Interim, fractional, or freshly promoted — you have to steer a technical team and evaluate work you can't fully understand.

A couple of minutes after takeoff, we started having engine trouble. We were going to crash — the only question was how and where… I knew I was going to die. One of the things I considered in the few seconds I had left was what I would leave behind… The lessons I had learned from my years in engineering and as a six-time CTO would all vanish. This book is my attempt at sharing them.

— From the Preface

About the author

Scott Darden has spent thirty years in engineering and served six times as CTO, building and leading teams across startups and enterprises. In 2019 he barely survived a plane crash while flight-testing a radar his company built — the experience that became the reason this book exists. He writes for the leaders who manage engineers without being one.
