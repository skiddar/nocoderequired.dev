Further Reading

Every book recommended in No Code Required, gathered in one place — 48 titles on engineering leadership and teams, plus a biography of the engineer behind each chapter.

This page contains affiliate links. As an Amazon Associate, the author earns from qualifying purchases — at no extra cost to you.

Chapter 1 · Your Team’s Culture

The Hard Thing About Hard Things Ben Horowitz

Startup culture from the inside. Horowitz shares hard-won lessons about building and maintaining culture through crises and growth.

Dealers of Lightning Michael Hiltzik

The story of Xerox PARC, where the future of computing was invented — and how culture enabled (and squandered) that innovation.

An Elegant Puzzle Will Larson

Systems thinking applied to engineering culture and organization design.

On Robert NoyceThe Man Behind the Microchip Leslie Berlin

How the co-inventor of the integrated circuit built the culture and risk-taking ethos that defined Silicon Valley.

Chapter 2 · Your Team’s Composition

The Mythical Man-Month Fred Brooks

The foundational text on software team dynamics. Introduces Brooks’s Law (adding people to a late project makes it later) and documents the 10:1 productivity variance between engineers — essential context for understanding team composition.

Team Topologies Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais

A modern framework for organizing engineering teams around four fundamental types and three interaction modes. Explains why team structure determines system architecture.

Peopleware Tom DeMarco & Timothy Lister

The classic on human factors in software teams. Argues that major development problems are human, not technical — covering culture, environment, and what makes teams gel.

On Neil ArmstrongFirst Man James R. Hansen

The only authorized biography, drawing on over 50 hours of interviews to reveal the private man behind the first Moon landing.

Chapter 3 · Your Team’s Process

Lean Software Development Mary & Tom Poppendieck

Agile principles from a lean manufacturing perspective. Connects software process to the Wright Brothers’ efficiency mindset.

Extreme Programming Explained Kent Beck

The original Agile methodology explained by its creator. Foundational for understanding where modern practices come from.

The Goal Eliyahu Goldratt

Theory of Constraints as a novel. Explains bottlenecks and process optimization in an unexpectedly engaging format.

On the Wright BrothersThe Wright Brothers David McCullough

Pulitzer Prize-winning historian draws on private family papers to tell how two self-taught Ohio brothers achieved powered flight through relentless experimentation.

Chapter 4 · Your Team’s Output

Code Complete Steve McConnell

Comprehensive guide to the software development process. Called “the most practical guide to programming” and still essential decades later.

A Philosophy of Software Design John Ousterhout

A Stanford professor’s framework for thinking about software complexity. Surprisingly accessible for non-engineers.

The Art of Readable Code Dustin Boswell & Trevor Foucher

Practical techniques for writing code that others can understand. Short, actionable, and explains why readability matters.

On Margaret HamiltonThe Woman in the Moon Richard Maurer

The most thorough biography of Hamilton, featuring exclusive interviews covering her path from curious student to the software engineer who helped land astronauts on the Moon.

Chapter 5 · Establishing Trust & Respect

Radical Candor Kim Scott

How to be honest with your team while showing you care. The framework: care personally, challenge directly.

Leaders Eat Last Simon Sinek

Why great leaders sacrifice for their teams. Directly relevant to protecting your team from distractions and politics.

Crucial Conversations Patterson, Grenny, McMillan & Switzler

How to maintain trust and respect during difficult discussions when stakes are high.

On John Paul StappSonic Wind Craig Ryan

The biography of the Air Force physician who rode rocket sleds at speeds of 632 mph to gather crash-survival data that revolutionized automobile and aircraft safety.

Chapter 6 · Directing Your Team

High Output Management Andrew S. Grove

Intel CEO’s management classic. The foundation for understanding how to direct without micromanaging.

First, Break All the Rules Marcus Buckingham & Curt Coffman

Gallup research on what great managers do differently. Focus on strengths, not weaknesses.

Measure What Matters John Doerr

OKRs explained by the person who brought them to Google. How to set clear, measurable goals.

On Frederick Winslow TaylorThe One Best Way Robert Kanigel

The definitive biography of the father of scientific management, whose obsessive pursuit of efficiency transformed modern industry.

Chapter 7 · Outsourcing Development

Remote: Office Not Required Jason Fried & David Heinemeier Hansson

Basecamp founders on distributed work. The case for remote teams and how to make them succeed.

Synergea Andy Hilliard

A blueprint for building effective globally distributed teams in software development. Highly specific to outsourcing.

Distributed Teams John O’Duinn

Practical, actionable guide for anyone working in, joining, or managing a distributed team.

On Dame Stephanie ShirleyLet It Go Dame Stephanie Shirley with Richard Askwith

Her autobiography, from Kindertransport refugee to founder of a pioneering all-women software company that made seventy of her staff millionaires.

Chapter 8 · Realistic Expectations

Software Estimation: Demystifying the Black Art Steve McConnell

THE book on software estimation. Practical, tested, real-world advice for understanding how long things actually take.

Software Estimation Without Guessing George Dinwiddie

Modern take on estimation. Helps identify the real needs behind estimate requests.

Waltzing with Bears Tom DeMarco & Timothy Lister

Managing risk in software projects. Why uncertainty is inherent and how to plan for it.

On Claude ShannonA Mind at Play Jimmy Soni & Rob Goodman

The first full biography of the mathematician whose foundational work in information theory created the intellectual framework for the digital age.

Chapter 9 · Better and Faster Decisions

Thinking in Bets Annie Duke

Former poker champion on decision-making under uncertainty. Highly practical for weighing probabilities and learning from outcomes.

Decisive Chip & Dan Heath

The WRAP framework: Widen options, Reality-test assumptions, Attain distance, Prepare to be wrong.

Good Strategy Bad Strategy Richard Rumelt

What makes strategy actually good. Diagnosis, guiding policy, and coherent action.

On Nikola TeslaTesla: Inventor of the Electrical Age W. Bernard Carlson

A scholarly yet accessible biography that shows how Tesla combined idealistic vision with practical engineering to reshape the electrical industry.

Chapter 10 · Hiring Great People

The Holloway Guide to Technical Recruiting and Hiring Osman et al

Comprehensive guide by hiring managers from Quora, Dropbox, and Google. The most specific resource for technical hiring.

Who Geoff Smart & Randy Street

The A-Method for hiring. A structured approach to avoid costly hiring mistakes.

Work Rules! Laszlo Bock

Google’s head of People Operations on hiring and culture. Data-driven approach to building great teams.

On Gordon MooreMoore’s Law Arnold Thackray, David C. Brock & Rachel Jones

The authorized biography of Intel’s co-founder, whose famous observation about transistor density became the self-fulfilling prophecy driving the semiconductor industry.

Chapter 11 · Motivating Your Team

Drive Daniel Pink

The science of intrinsic motivation: autonomy, mastery, and purpose. Essential reading for understanding what actually motivates engineers.

Punished by Rewards Alfie Kohn

Why incentive systems often backfire. Challenges traditional thinking about rewards and motivation.

The Culture Code Daniel Coyle

How highly successful groups build belonging, safety, and shared purpose. Practical insights for team motivation.

On Grace HopperGrace Hopper and the Invention of the Information Age Kurt W. Beyer

How Hopper’s work on compilers and programming languages — enabling humans to communicate with computers in everyday terms — laid the foundation for modern software development.

Chapter 12 · Force Multipliers

Accelerate Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble & Gene Kim

Data-driven analysis of what makes high-performing technology organizations. The research behind DevOps metrics and continuous delivery as force multipliers.

The Alignment Problem Brian Christian

How AI learns human values — and how it can go wrong. Essential context for managing AI-augmented teams.

Human + Machine Paul R. Daugherty & H. James Wilson

Based on research with 1,500 organizations, a practical guide to redesigning work so that humans and AI collaborate as force multipliers.

On Fei-Fei LiThe Worlds I See Fei

Fei Li — The story of ImageNet and the AI revolution, from curiosity to breakthrough.

These are the shoulders No Code Required stands on.
